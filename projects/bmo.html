BMO

BMO is a compact interactive-device concept with a screen, physical controls, and expressive form inspired by retro handheld electronics.

Gallery

Project Details

Product Concept Embedded Interface 3D Modeling Mechanical Layout

System Architecture

The concept includes a main enclosure with front display cutout, tactile control cluster, side perforations for audio output, and articulated rod-like side elements designed as expressive mechanical features.

Technical Decisions

The top panel was arranged to keep controls within thumb reach while reserving the largest area for the display. Rounded corners and a single-shell body were chosen to simplify fabrication and improve handheld comfort.

Challenges

Main challenges included fitting multiple interface elements on a compact top face, keeping mechanical clearances around protruding side components, and maintaining structural rigidity around openings and cutouts.

Results

The final model achieved a coherent retro-robot identity, practical control placement, and a layout that can be adapted into a functional embedded prototype with display and input hardware.

Highlights

  • Designed a compact enclosure with integrated screen and controls.
  • Added robot-inspired mechanical features for visual character.
  • Defined a layout suitable for handheld embedded interaction.
  • Prepared concept geometry for next-stage prototyping.
