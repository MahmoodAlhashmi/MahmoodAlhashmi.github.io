Date Processing Conveyor Line

Designed, modeled, wired, and tested an automated acrylic date processing line that feeds, cleans, dries, inspects, fills, and stamps packaged dates.

Project Details

Machine DesignAutomationComputer VisionMLEmbedded Systems

System Architecture

The process begins with date storage feeding into an Archimedes screw that delivers fruit onto the conveyor. A rotating cleaning stage sprays fine mist water while shaking and flipping the dates. Two ducted fans dry the dates before an AI-based inspection stage classifies good and defective pieces using a custom machine learning model. Accepted dates are dropped into paper cups, filled with one of several sauces, sealed with lids, and stamped with the selected flavor and logo.

Technical Decisions

Acrylic was used for the structure because it is lightweight, easy to fabricate, and visually exposes the internal process. The Archimedes screw provided controlled feeding from storage. The rotating cleaner combined agitation and misting in one compact mechanism. AI inspection added quality control before packaging. Interactive cup and lid placement made the prototype more engaging for children.

Challenges

The main challenges included feeding dates smoothly without damage, cleaning them effectively while rotating and flipping them, drying them sufficiently before packaging, integrating AI-based inspection into the workflow, and coordinating dropping, filling, and stamping timing.

Results

The final prototype demonstrated a complete workflow including feeding, cleaning, drying, AI-based inspection, filling, and stamping. It combined mechanical design, wiring, testing, and intelligent inspection into one working educational and industrial-style prototype.

Highlights

  • Used an Archimedes screw for controlled feeding from storage to conveyor.
  • Integrated cleaning, drying, and packaging stages into one working system.
  • Added AI-based inspection with a custom model to classify good and defective dates.
  • Included interactive filling and packaging steps for children.
