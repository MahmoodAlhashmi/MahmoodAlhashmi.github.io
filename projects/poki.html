Poki

Poki is a compact companion-device concept that combines playful industrial design with an embedded display interface in a cat-ear form factor.

Gallery

Project Details

Product Concept Industrial Design 3D Modeling Embedded Display

System Architecture

The design consists of a rounded protective shell with integrated cat-ear geometry, a front display window for facial expression output, and an internal cavity sized for compact embedded electronics and power components.

Technical Decisions

The enclosure was shaped to balance visual identity, visibility of the display, and usable internal volume. A front-focused screen area was prioritized to make the interface readable while keeping the body compact and lightweight.

Challenges

Key challenges were maintaining a clean outer form while preserving mounting space for electronics, ensuring front opening dimensions matched display constraints, and keeping the geometry practical for fabrication.

Results

The final concept produced a clear cat-inspired identity, a workable electronics enclosure layout, and a design path ready for physical prototyping and embedded integration.

Highlights

  • Created a cat-themed enclosure with integrated display window.
  • Explored both rendered and CAD concept views of the same design.
  • Balanced style, internal volume, and fabrication practicality.
  • Prepared the concept for future electronics integration.
